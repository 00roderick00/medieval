## 1. Henry VII's playbook

**Chamber finance. [CONSENSUS on the institution; series gated]** From c.
1487 the King's Chamber displaced the Exchequer as the working treasury —
"in effect, a national treasury" (Tudor Chamber Books project, T2) — under
Treasurers Lovell (1485–92) and Heron (1492–1509), with the king's own
sign manual on the books: Henry "personally annotated the chamber account
books… signing off the payments weekly," auditing with the clerk roughly
quarterly (T2; the every-page-initialled legend overstates). Revenue
figures circulating openly (crown lands £12k → £42k; customs ~£33k →
~£40k; total ~£100k p.a.) are tertiary and ultimately Wolffe's/Chrimes's —
**[UNVERIFIED — RETRIEVAL]**; Grummitt (1999, gated) confirms from the
Heron books that "stories of Henry's great wealth were not fables of Tudor
propaganda" while resituating the Yorkist continuity. **[BOOK'S ARGUMENT]**
The Chamber is the fiscal move in its English form: revenue routed to an
office the king audits in person — the fisc personalised in order to be
institutionalised.

**Bonds and recognizances. [CONSENSUS, figures per §0.2]** Lander's
tallies (as corrected): 36/62 peerage families bound; ~46/62 under some
coercive instrument. Cunningham (2009) reframes: recognizances as an
*allegiance system*, not mere fiscal terror — name both readings. The
**Council Learned in the Law** (c. 1495; Bray, then Empson and Dudley)
administered the machine without appeal; Henry VIII opened his reign by
arresting both ministers (24 April 1509) and executing them (17 August
1510) — the successor sacrificing the father's instruments while keeping
the father's machine. **The Bergavenny case (Ross 2021, open, T2)** is the
mechanism in miniature: 471 retainers indicted under the **1504 statute**
(retaining lawful only "by the vertue of the kynges plagart"), £70,650
assessed, commuted to £500/yr plus a geographic exclusion, cancelled by
the new king — the fine never meant to be collected, only to convert a
magnate into a bondholder of his own obedience. Cross-ref ch03 §1 (the
1504 licensing monopoly) and ch08 (Star Chamber correction maintained).

## 2. Louis XI, extended

**[Carried from ch10, reviewed]** The French limb is established: the
tripled taille, the standing companies, the artillery at 8% of military
expenditure (1489), the appanages absorbed, Burgundy swallowed (1477/82).
The extension here is the *mature apparatus abroad*: Charles VIII's 1494
Italian expedition — Giovio's "more than 36 pieces that the horses drew at
an incredible speed"; Contamine's verdict that Charles believed he
"possédait un instrument qu'aucune coalition ne pouvait briser" (1964, p.
261, in repo) — the fiscal-military state's first continental adventure,
executed by the institutions chs. 6 and 10 documented. **[CONSENSUS, in
repo]**

## 3. Ferdinand and Isabella

**The Hermandad. [CONSENSUS]** Cortes of Madrigal, 1476: the local
hermandades reorganised into a crown-run constabulary under a royal
council; it policed the roads and *funded the Granada war*; the central
council was dissolved in 1498 once the work was done (Lunenfeld, T2 via
review; detail gated). Domestication of local order as a *temporary royal
instrument* — stood up, used, wound down.

**The military orders — the two-step. [CONSENSUS]** Calatrava's
administration to Ferdinand on the master's death (1487); Alcántara by
resignation under a 1492 bull (effective 1494); Santiago in **1499**;
then, under Charles V, **Adrian VI's bull (1523) annexing all three
masterships to the crown of Castile in perpetuity**. Papal instruments
granting the king *administration* personally; a generation later, the
permanent absorption. The wealthiest corporate militaries in Iberia became
crown patronage without a battle. **[BOOK'S ARGUMENT]** This is the
chapter's cleanest medieval case of absorption-not-destruction — the
Henrician move avant la lettre, executed through the rival universalism's
own legal forms.

**The Inquisition. [CONSENSUS]** Sixtus IV's bull *Exigit sincerae
devotionis* (1 November 1478) "gave the monarchs exclusive authority to
name the inquisitors" — canonical machinery under royal appointment;
first tribunal Seville (1480–81); Torquemada Inquisitor General from 1483,
extending one royally-appointed hierarchy across Castile *and* Aragon —
the only institution besides the monarchy operating in all the Spanish
kingdoms. The canonical-administrative fusion, documented at the level of
the founding bull. Scale/victim numbers: **[UNVERIFIED — RETRIEVAL:
Kamen; Contreras & Henningsen]** — the draft asserts the institution, not
casualty figures. The **1492 Alhambra Decree** (text open in translation,
T1) and the **1502** conversion pragmatic are the uniformity-legibility
moves; expulsion/conversion numbers are contested ranges — flag.
Revenue: the circulating 900,000 reales (1474) → 26 million (1504) arc is
Elliott's via tertiary echo — **[UNVERIFIED — RETRIEVAL: Elliott; Ladero
Quesada]**.

## 4. The Ottoman variant — the most radical

**The devşirme. [CONSENSUS mechanism; numbers hedged per §0.3]** The levy
of Christian boys (canonically one per forty households; periodicity
varying with need), converted, cut from family, trained into the
Janissaries or through the palace schools into the administration — the
polity manufacturing its service class from raw material rather than
negotiating with a hereditary nobility. Corps size under Mehmed II:
"roughly five to ten thousand," register-based counts at the low end.
İnalcık's framing (the *kul* system as the engine of Ottoman centralism)
remains **[UNVERIFIED — RETRIEVAL]**, carried meanwhile by open T2:
after 1453 "Ottoman sultans stopped appointing scholars to vizierial
positions, preferring viziers of kul… origin" (*Belleten*, 2025, open).

**Mehmed II's consolidation. [CONSENSUS]** The execution of Grand Vizier
**Çandarlı Halil Pasha (1 June 1453)** — the first grand vizier ever
executed, last of the old aristocratic vizieral dynasty, replaced by the
kul Zaganos Pasha: the over-mighty-subject problem solved by decapitating
the office's aristocratic tenure itself. The **kanunname** (with the
fratricide clause: "Fratricide, for nizām-i 'ālem, is acceptable…") —
authenticity debate staged per §0.3(c) (Ekinci, *Belleten* 2018, open).
The land measure — mülk and vakıf conversions to state land for timar
redistribution — with **Özel's open revisionist caveat** (revenue-status
change more than expropriation) and Bayezid II's partial restoration.
**[BOOK'S ARGUMENT]** The Ottoman case is in the chapter because it is
the *limit case*: where Henry VII bonded his magnates and Ferdinand
absorbed his orders, Mehmed manufactured a service class with no
independent base at all — the most complete answer to the over-mighty
subject the fifteenth century produced, achieved by an imperial-household
logic the Latin monarchies never possessed (the disanalogy, stated).

## 5. Print, and the state swallowing the Church

**The shock. [CONSENSUS]** Gutenberg's press at Mainz, the 42-line Bible
complete by 1455; presses in **roughly 250–280 towns by 1500** (open
counts vary 236–282); the ISTC records ~27,500 incunabula editions;
total output "somewhere around 15 to 20 million copies" (Britannica; the
20M formulation is Febvre & Martin's — **[RETRIEVAL]** for the book).

**The demonstration that print was politically decisive. [T2, open]**
Edwards, *Printing, Propaganda, and Martin Luther* (full text open):
Luther's works were **~20% of all pamphlets published 1500–1530**, and
German presses "produced nearly five times as many German works by Luther
as by all the Catholic controversialists put together." **The Henrician
harness [CONSENSUS, T1/T2]:** the Act of Supremacy (November 1534);
Cromwell's injunctions (5 September 1538) ordering "one book of the whole
Bible of the largest volume in English" into every parish; the **Great
Bible (April 1539)**; the royal proclamation of **16 November 1538**
requiring pre-publication licensing — the state simultaneously
*mandating* the vernacular canon and *licensing* the means of its
reproduction; the Stationers' charter (1557) delegating enforcement to a
chartered guild; Rome's mirror-move, the Index (1559). **[BOOK'S
ARGUMENT]** Print did for the sixteenth-century state what artillery had
done for the fifteenth: changed the economics of political community —
and only polities that could *license, mandate, and administer* the new
medium at national scale could exploit it. Henry VIII's supremacy was
executable because a national vernacular religion had become
*administrable*. **Historiographical discipline:** the Eisenstein–Johns
debate is named (fixity as inherent vs socially made; the 2002 *AHR*
forum); the chapter's claim survives on the weaker ground both sides
concede — print was used, massively and successfully, as an instrument of
doctrinal administration; whether "print culture" did the work is the
contested surplus. (The sceptics-concede thread, print edition.)

## 6. The five moves, restated

**[BOOK'S ARGUMENT (c)]** Fiscal reform (permanent central taxation with
administrative apparatus); subordination of great subjects; domestication
of the market for force; canonical assertion over rival jurisdictions;
mobilisation of communications technology for legibility. Between c. 1470
and 1530 England, France, Spain and the Ottoman state each executed
variants (the Habsburg composite following differently); the pattern is
the book's synthesis, its ancestry in the contested "New Monarchies"
historiography declared (§0.1); the Ottoman extension is the book's own.

## 7. The predictive translation — the five moves now (all dated)

**Move 1 — fiscal reform. [T1 core; one large complication]** The
apparatus is real: CRS/AEOI at **116 jurisdictions exchanging information
on 171 million accounts, ~EUR 13 trillion (2024)** (OECD 2025 peer
review); Pillar Two live in ~55–60 jurisdictions from 2024–26 (EU
Directive 2022/2523; UK, Japan, Korea et al.); the EU's proposed
corporate lump-sum own-resource (CORE, July 2025); India's GST/e-invoice
legibility machine atop UPI (~20bn transactions/month, Aug 2025). **The
complication, printed not smoothed:** the OECD **Side-by-Side Package
(applying 1 Jan 2026) takes US-parented groups outside the IIR/UTPR**;
Canada rescinded its DST under tariff threat (June 2025); US 100%-tariff
threats against DST states continued into June 2026. **[BOOK'S ARGUMENT]**
Read structurally, this is not the fiscal move failing but its geometry:
consolidation is proceeding *bloc-internally* while the hegemon breaks
the *cross-bloc* harmonisation that would bind its own subjects — which
is precisely the parallel-settlements reading against the
global-governance one. The draft states both readings; critiques.md
Objection 3 carries the sceptical one.

**Move 2 — subordination of great subjects. [T1; with the state's losses
printed]** EU: DMA first fines (Apple €500m, Meta €200m, Apr 2025); the
General Court dismissing Apple's gatekeeper challenge (8 July 2026); DSA
first fine (X, **€120m**, 5 Dec 2025; appealed). US: the Google search
remedies (Sept 2025 — conduct remedies and compelled data-sharing, no
divestiture; cross-appeals into 2026); adtech remedies pending; **FTC v.
Meta lost** (Nov 2025; appealed). China: the 2020–23 crackdown closed out
as completed discipline (the Feb 2025 entrepreneurs' symposium; the
Private Economy Promotion Law, May 2025 — normalisation *after*
subordination; T3 pending browser pins). **[BOOK'S ARGUMENT]** The
pattern across blocs is discipline-through-process, with the state
sometimes losing in its own courts — which the chapter argues is the
settlement's *form*, not its refutation (Objection 3): Henry VII's
instruments were legal instruments, and the crown that rules through law
is also bound by it — hierarchical plurality (ch08) operating at the top.

**Move 3 — domestication of the market for force. [T1/T3]** Russia
completed the re-monopolisation: "On 6 June 2025, the government-
controlled Wagner Group announced leaving Mali, with the **Africa Corps**
continuing Russian operations" — the MoD's own vehicle, under a serving
general, absorbing the theatres (ch04's arc closed). The US pattern
(programmes of record, IL6, Detachment 201 — ch03/ch04) plus the CDAO
frontier-AI agreements ($200M ceilings, July 2025); the Montreux process
inching (61 states; CoE joined Feb 2026). **And the test case resolved in
a direction nobody scripted:** the presidential directive excluding
Anthropic (GSA, 27 Feb 2026: "IMMEDIATELY CEASE all use of Anthropic's
technology") was **reversed by preliminary injunction** (26 Mar 2026),
GSA restoring "the status quo in effect prior to February 27, 2026"
(3 Apr 2026, T1). The paymaster state tried the coercive instrument on
the one contractor-class actor with an independent fiscal base — and the
state's own courts stayed its hand. Cross-ref C3-2/C2-3; drafted as a
dated episode, litigation ongoing.

**Move 4 — canonical assertion. [T1; with the slippages printed]** EU:
prohibitions applied (Feb 2025), GPAI obligations applied (Aug 2025) with
the Code of Practice signed by OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Microsoft,
Mistral et al. (Meta refusing — T3 pin pending); **the high-risk tier

**Move 5 — communications/legibility mobilised. [T1/T3]** The

## 8. The central prediction, stated — with the Sforza distinction

**[BOOK'S ARGUMENT]** The state will not break the platforms; it will
nationalise their functions — by security-clearance and procurement
integration (US), canonical regulation (EU), fused ownership (China),
sovereign-stack substitution (India). The five moves are observable in
every bloc at the instrument level documented above; the interstitial
spaces stay neomedieval (chs. 8, 12). **The ch04 thread sharpens the
settlement's legal form:** absorption may leave the platforms *operative
but never invested* — running state infrastructure under licence, rank,
and programme of record without recognised standing (no golden shares;
revocable directives; Detachment 201 commissions) — Sforza ruling Milan
uninvested. The Anthropic episode shows the converse edge: the state
withholding investiture *and* being compelled by its own courts to keep
the relationship operative. Function delegated, legitimacy retained, on
both sides — that is what "leashed rather than destroyed" looks like in
documents. Forward-leaning close into ch12: who, then, is Henry VII —
and what is his artillery?
