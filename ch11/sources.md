## T2 — Canonical scholarship / specialist (open, consulted)

- **Ross**, "Lord Bergavenny's illegal retaining revisited, 1501–22,"
  *Historical Research* 94 (2021) — **open access**; the 471-man
  indictment, £70,650 assessment, £500/yr commutation, 1509
  cancellation, all verbatim.
- **Tudor Chamber Books project** (Winchester/TNA) — the Chamber as "in
  effect, a national treasury"; the sign manuals; weekly sign-off,
  quarterly audit.
- **Ekinci**, "Fratricide in Ottoman Law," *Belleten* 82 (2018) — the
  clause verbatim + the Dilger/Berki-vs-consensus authenticity debate.
- **Belleten (2025)**, "Rise of Indigenous Ottoman Viziers" — post-1453
  kul-vizier preference stated.
- **Özel**, "Limits of the Almighty: Mehmed II's 'Land Reform'
  Revisited," *JESHO* 42 (1999) — open copy; the revisionist caveat.
- **Edwards**, *Printing, Propaganda, and Martin Luther* — **full text
  open** (UC Press E-Books): Luther ~20% of pamphlets 1500–1530; 5:1
  against Catholic controversialists.
- **Copyright-history commentaries** (QUB project): the 1538
  proclamation and 1557 charter, scholarly and open.
- **Cunningham**, "Loyalty and the Usurper," *Historical Research* 82
  (2009) — recognizances as allegiance system (abstract/summary open).
- **Gunn**, "Politic history, New Monarchy and state formation," *HR* 82
  (2009) — abstract open (the historiographical foil, periodised);
  **Grummitt**, *HR* 72 (1999) — abstract open (chamber-finance
  revisionism).
- **Bernabé Pons**, "Expulsion of the Muslims from Spain" (EGO, open) —
  the 1502 pragmatic.
- **Lunenfeld** via *HAHR* review (open) — Hermandad 1476–1498.
- **EGO/duke/JMH 2023 open items** on Castilian finance (the 1475–79
  war-finance article, open at T&F).
