# Brief — Ch. 11: The New Monarchies (~13,000 words)
PURPOSE: The five moves, run comparatively, then translated. Henry VII
(chamber finance, Star Chamber, bonds and recognizances, Council Learned —
Gunn, Chrimes); Louis XI extended; Ferdinand and Isabella (hermandades,
military orders absorbed post-1476, Inquisition as canonical-administrative
fusion — Elliott); the devşirme as the most radical variant (İnalcık,
Kafadar); print as the second technology shock and Henry VIII's swallowing
of the Church (Eisenstein) — the move that most prefigures the present.
The predictive translation of each move to current evidence (Pillar
Two/CRS/FATCA; antitrust wave and platform crackdown; PMSC regularisation
and post-Prigozhin Russia; DMA/DSA/AI Act; sovereign AI, CBDCs, India
Stack, GAIA-X). The central prediction stated plainly: not breaking but
nationalising the platforms' functions, differently per bloc.
RESEARCH FOCUS: Gunn, Chrimes, Elliott, İnalcık, Kafadar, Eisenstein,
Elton (retrieval); contemporary primary sources per move (open).
COMPARATORS: England, France, Spain, the Ottomans, the Habsburgs; all four
modern blocs.
OBJECTION (critiques.md): both outline objections — the great-power-frame
complaint (answered by the internal/external test) and the libertarian
complaint (countervailing tech operates in the interstices).
