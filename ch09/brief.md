# Brief — Ch. 9: The King's Two Bodies, Broken (~11,000 words)
PURPOSE: The philosophical peak of Part II. Kantorowicz re-read; Henry VI's
incapacity as two-body crisis; Charles VI as the deeper case (intermittency
prevented stable regency); the regency experiments and their consistent
failure; Elton's Tudor Revolution engaged with Starkey/Coleman critiques —
the weak thesis suffices; modern personalisation (presidentialisation,
founder-CEO sovereignty, Musk/Zuckerberg/Xi as two-body problems) and the
improvised institutional substitutes; indicators of the pending
institutionalisation (trust-and-safety professionalisation, institutional
AI governance, routinised executive doctrine).
RESEARCH FOCUS: Kantorowicz, Elton, Starkey, Coleman & Starkey, Watts,
Famiglietti, Guenée (retrieval).
COMPARATORS: England, France; platform polities for the modern layer.
OBJECTION (critiques.md): the Elton-revisionist complaint — the argument
requires only the personal-to-institutional transition, which is
uncontested.
