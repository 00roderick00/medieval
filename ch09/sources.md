## T1 — Primary sources (open, consulted; key passages extracted verbatim)

- **Edmund Plowden, *Commentaries or Reports*** (1816 English ed., public
  domain, archive.org) — the Case of the Duchy of Lancaster (4 Eliz., 1561),
  Plowden 212a–213: the two-bodies formulation, verbatim (one page-break
  phrase to check against page images); the marginal "always adjudged in Law
  of full Age."
- ***Rotuli Parliamentorum*, vol. V** (1767 ed., public domain, archive.org)
  — pp. 240–42: the Windsor deputation (25 Mar 1454); the election
  "Protectorem & Defensorem… quamdiu Regi placeret"; York's protestation;
  the negative definition of the office ("nought the name of Tutour…").
- **Blackstone, *Commentaries*, Bk I ch. 7** (Avalon Project, open) — "The
  king never dies"; the demise-of-the-crown doctrine citing Plowden.
- **F. W. Maitland, "The Crown as Corporation" (1901)**, *Collected Papers*
  III (1911, public domain, archive.org) — the sceptical precursor.
- **Treaty of Troyes (1420)** — Fordham Internet Medieval Sourcebook (Ogg
  1907 translation, public domain): clauses 6, 7, 24, 29 verified.
- ***Ordonnances des rois de France*, vols. VII–VIII** — the Jan 1393 and
  Apr 1403 regency ordinances, via Adams's CC-licensed chapter (footnoted to
  the printed volumes; BnF public-domain scans catalogued).
- **SEC filings (EDGAR):** Meta DEF 14A 2026 (Zuckerberg 60.8% voting power;
  "controlled company" language; pledged shares) and 2025 (61.0%); Alphabet
  DEF 14A 2026 (Page 27.4% + Brin 25.3%); Tesla DEF 14A 2025 (Musk 19.8%
  beneficial). Verbatim extractions in the research record.
- **EUR-Lex, Regulation (EU) 2022/2065 (DSA), Article 41** — the mandated
  independent compliance function, verbatim.
- **Executive Order establishing DOGE (20 Jan 2025)** — whitehouse.gov /
  Federal Register.
- **SEC Staff Legal Bulletin No. 14E (2009)** — CEO succession reclassified
  as a governance issue, verbatim.
- **American Presidency Project (UCSB), executive-orders table** (open
  primary data; updated Jul 2026) — Trump II at 179 EOs/year.
- **Jay Ritter, dual-class IPO dataset** (Univ. of Florida, open; Feb 2026
  update) — 48.4% of 2025 tech IPOs dual-class (Table 23).
- **Anthropic Long-Term Benefit Trust announcement** (19 Sep 2023, primary).

## T2 — Canonical scholarship / specialist (open, consulted)

- **J. R. Lander, "Henry VI and the Duke of York's Second Protectorate,"
  *BJRL* 43:1 (1960)** — open access (CC), quoting *Rot. Parl.* v verbatim;
  the constitutional-form anchor for 1454/1455–56.
- **Patrick Collinson, "Geoffrey Rudolph Elton 1921–1994," *PBA* 94 (1997)**
  — open PDF; carries the Tudor-revolution narrative, the *P&P* debate
  shape, Starkey's counter, and Elton's 1991 concession, with page cites.
- **Robert Folz (1958) and Jean Imbert (1961) reviews of Kantorowicz** —
  open on Persée; confirm the book's arc and reception.
- **Kenneth Vickers, *Humphrey Duke of Gloucester*** (1907, public domain,
  Gutenberg #41477) — the 1422 minority arrangements (dated scholarship;
  facts rest on the rolls; check emphases against Griffiths/Watts).
- **Tracy Adams, "Medieval Mothers and Their Children…"** (De Gruyter 2005,
  CC via OAPEN) — the 1393/1403 French regency ordinances.
- **Alice Miller, "More Already on the Central Committee's Leading Small
  Groups," *China Leadership Monitor* 44** (Hoover, open PDF) — Xi chairing
  six of eleven LSGs; their off-the-books character.
- **Julio Juan Ruiz (2024, open access)** — the *corpus mysticum* transfer;
  **Wimmer (2014, OA)**; **JHIBlog** retrospectives.
- **History of Parliament Trust blog (Kleineke)** — 1426 "Parliament of

- **Reviews in History** (IHR, open): Horowitz (no. 1168) on Cromwell;
