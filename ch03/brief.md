# Brief — Ch. 3: Bastard Feudalism, Inc. (~11,000 words)
PURPOSE: The contractor economy, medieval and modern. Comparative medieval
original: English livery and maintenance (McFarlane's affinity); French
écorcheurs; Burgundian clientage (Vaughan); German Fehde/Landfrieden; Italy
deferred to ch. 4. Modern parallel: the PMSC industry (McFate), extended to
intelligence contracting, border security, logistics, cyber, AI-mediated
decision support. Williams's dark-age counter-read integrated. The twin
mechanisms: retention (indenture → contract/clearance/ITAR) and finance —
the crucial disanalogy that modern contractors lack independent fiscal
bases; frontier AI labs flagged as the anomaly. Prigozhin noted, developed
in ch. 4.
RESEARCH FOCUS: McFarlane, Vaughan, Contamine (retrieval); McFate thesis,
Williams, Cerny 2005 (bundled); contemporary industry reporting on PMSCs
and the AI-lab-to-state relationship.
COMPARATORS: England, France, Burgundy, the Empire.
OBJECTION (critiques.md): the monopoly-not-eroded complaint — conceded on
dependence, sharpened into the AI-lab anomaly.
