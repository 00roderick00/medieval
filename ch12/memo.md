## 1. The settlement from outside

**[BOOK'S ARGUMENT, components sourced]** Three or four parallel
consolidations, each Tudor in structure, Westphalian only in silhouette;
durable disorder persisting in the gaps (Cerny's conclusion, in repo;

grey zones with their untaxed fraction shrinking). The mid-2026 markers,

commit to invest 5% of GDP annually on core defence requirements as well
as defence- and security-related spending by 2035" (3.5% + 1.5%; T1);
**ReArm Europe / Readiness 2030** (announced 4 Mar 2025; up to €800bn —
€650bn fiscal flexibility + €150bn SAFE loans; SAFE disbursing from

"greater self-reliance and strength in science and technology" (T1);
the US 2026 chip-policy turn (eased China licensing paired with a
Section 232 tariff on imported advanced chips — T3/T4, browser pins
owed). Exogenous territorialising pressure — the variable Cerny declared
absent in 1998 — is back on the books of every bloc at once (ch01's
characteristic-2 turn, completed).

## 2. The settlement from inside

**[BOOK'S ARGUMENT, instruments documented in the owning chapters]** The
lived order the chapters have assembled: **hierarchically plural law** —
state courts above platform process above private ordering, with the
hierarchy now statutory (DSA Arts. 20–21: compulsory internal process,
certified external bodies expressly denied binding power — ch08) and
judicially enforced in both directions (the state fining platforms; the

with an international operations centre; digital identity stacks — the
persistent low-grade legibility the outline says a medieval subject
would have recognised only under the Inquisition's or the devşirme's
reach (ch11 §§3–4 — with the disanalogy stated: registration, not
levy; audit, not autos-da-fé; the register is [ANALOGY-ONLY] beyond the

**Personalisation atop bureaucracy** (ch09, pending review): dual-class
charters as the default constitution of the new institutions; DSA Art.
41's compelled independent compliance function; succession reclassified
as governance — the two-body problem re-clothed. **And the settlement's
legal form for the absorbed platforms — the Sforza distinction (ch04 →
ch11):** operative but never invested; function delegated through
licence, rank, clearance and programme of record; legitimacy retained by
the state — with the Anthropic arc showing the courts holding *both*
sides of that bargain in place.

## 3. Who is Henry VII, and what is his artillery?

**The United States — the most probable case. [Components T1; casting
(c)]** Five moves, executed haphazardly and half-consciously: the fisc
extended through its own instruments while exempted from everyone
else's (GENIUS rails; the Pillar Two carve-out read from the inside);
subordination through its own courts, wins and losses alike (Google
remedies; FTC's Meta loss on appeal; the contempt line against Apple);
force domesticated by procurement-and-personnel (programmes of record,
IL6, Detachment 201, CDAO ceilings) with no golden share — and the
Anthropic episode as the live boundary case; canonical assertion turned
inward (EO 14365 preempting the states) as much as outward (the AI
stack export EO); communications mobilised through OpenAI-for-Government,
USAi.gov, and the $1 deals. Its artillery: the stack ch10 documented —
CHIPS with an equity stake in the national champion, Secure Enclave, El
Capitan's deterrent mission, Starshield and the PWSA, the munitions ramp
with its audited shortfall. **China — substantially achieved. [ch02,
ch10, ch11]** The crackdown as completed discipline (Gregorian moment →
normalisation: the Feb 2025 symposium; the Private Economy Promotion
Law); fused ownership as the absorption mode; e-CNY and the data laws as
legibility; WAICO (29 states, 16 July 2026) as the canon exported; Big
Fund III, SMIC's EUV-less 7nm, Qianfan/Guowang as the state-directed
stack. **The European Union — canonically strong, structurally unstable.
[The Depreter refinement applied]** The most developed canonical
apparatus on earth (DMA/DSA/AI Act; the GC's gatekeeper ruling) and the
weakest fiscal-military substrate of the four: Magdeburg cancelled;
IRIS² at ~2030; ASAP counting capacity, not output; the AI Act's
high-risk tier delayed to 2027/28; gigafactories unsited; the digital
euro at 2029 pending 2026 legislation; ~€4.5bn of the "€43bn" directly
Commission-controlled. Ch10's Burgundy taught that apparatus without a
continuous dynastic-political centre does not survive contact; the EU is
the harder case — **apparatus-building without ever having possessed the
centre**: no single fisc, no single executive, its "dynasty" a treaty.
The chapter's assessment therefore stands as the outline wrote it, now
with a mechanism behind it: complete the settlement (ReArm, the 5%
pledge, SAFE, CORE are the observable attempts to build the substrate
under external pressure — the *exogenous* consolidator Cerny said was
missing) or become the American bloc's canonical province. The test is
Appendix C's: whether assertion-to-enforcement lag closes as the fisc
arrives. **India — the possible fourth. [ch10 §7, ch11 §7]** The
legibility stack running ahead of the military one: UPI/Aadhaar/GST as
the most complete population-legibility instrument outside China; ISM
2.0; Dholera at 28nm-class; ISRO at ~1/20th of NASA's budget.
"Possibly" — conditional, as the outline holds. **Everyone else**:
clients of one of the four, or inhabitants of the interstices — ch06's
capitulation-without-siege as procurement policy (C6-3).

## 4. The military-technology question, answered

**The low end has decentralised — the record is now quantified.
[Attributed throughout per §0.2]** Ukraine produced **2.2M drones in
2024** (Zelensky, 23 Feb 2025) and received ~**3M FPVs in 2025**
(Shmyhal, 24 Dec 2025) against a 4.5M procurement plan; basic FPVs run
~$300–400 (Forbes/Hambling) to ~$580 programme-average; drones account
for 60–70% of destroyed Russian *systems* (RUSI), ~70% of *casualties*
(Kostenko via NYT; medics via Politico), >60% of *strikes* (Syrskyi).
The longbow is real, cheap, and everywhere.

**And the longbow at scale is itself an industrial-state product — the
first half of the answer. [T2]** Russia's Shahed campaign is CSIS's
"drone saturation": launches rising "from approximately 200 per week to
more than 1,000 per week by March 2025"; the Alabuga plant documents a
6,000-drone production target (ISIS, from leaked documents); the
805-drone night of 6–7 Sept 2025 was "the largest attack since the
beginning of the full-scale invasion" (Ukrainian Air Force); 500-drone
nights are now routine. Both sides' "cheap" drone forces are
mass-production programmes of fiscal-military states — the modern
*francs-archers*, raised by the parish but paid for and standardised by
the crown. Cheapness per unit does not mean cheapness as a system
(Vershinin: attritional war "test[s] a country's industrial capacity").

**The decisive layer has concentrated — and Ukraine is the natural
experiment. [Dated, attributed]** The week of 3–11 March 2025: US
military aid suspended (3rd), intelligence sharing paused (~5th;
Ratcliffe's own "pause"), NGA cutting access to US-purchased commercial
imagery (8th) — HIMARS targeting and Patriot cueing degraded within
days, restoration following the Jeddah statement (11th). A drone-rich
army discovered, in one week, that its longbows aim through another
polity's cannon layer. The standing dependencies: ~42,000 Starlink
terminals as "the essential backbone of communication"; Patriot
interceptors at "about 25% of the... stockpile the Pentagon's plans
require" (Guardian, July 2025) with production at ~550–650/yr;
deep-strike permissions (ATACMS/Storm Shadow, Nov 2024) granted and
revocable by patrons; the Flamingo missile as a real but partial
sovereign substitute (production claims are the maker's — flagged).
**The analytical statement, quotable [T2]:** Bronk & Watling — UAVs
"may redistribute the balance of missions... but **they do not
eliminate the requirement for traditional artillery**," and a mass
precision strike complex "is not just a question of UAVs... EW,
geospatial and other ISR assets are fundamental." The counter-position
is staged fairly: Hammes's "small, smart, many" versus the few and
exquisite — and answered on the outline's two fronts: Ukraine's longbow
has raised the price of aggression without defeating a full-stack
adversary; and the cheap layer's own scaling re-concentrates it into
industrial policy. Open-source/distributed-compute erosion of the
decisive layer remains possible, not evident; C2-3/C3-2 keep it
scoreable. **[BOOK'S ARGUMENT]** Our decisive military technology is
asymmetrically cannon: distributed denial below, concentrated decision
above — the Cade rebellions and the artillery states of our age,
simultaneously.

## 6. The implications — directional only

**[Per CLAUDE.md §9: comparative historical analysis; asset-class
direction permitted, actionable guidance prohibited — the memo states
the boundary and the draft keeps it.]** The historical pattern the
chapter may state: Tudor-style settlements repriced whole classes of
position — monastic wealth dissolved and redistributed inside a decade;
franchise and liberty jurisdictions extinguished; chartered-company
forms created where the crown wanted capacity it could licence. The
modern directional analogues, stated as pattern not advice: value
migrates toward bloc-interior, licensable, procurement-adjacent
positions and away from cross-bloc arbitrage and interstitial
jurisdictions; compliance and reporting burdens function as the new
tithe; the licensing premium (clearances, gatekeeper status, charters)
becomes a durable rent; interstitial assets carry durable-disorder risk
that consolidation does not remove but *fences*. For the constituencies
the author briefs — insurers, endowments, sovereign funds, family
offices, strategic services — the chapter offers the fifteenth century's
ledger as the reference class, and nothing more specific.

## 7. Closing image — Castillon returned

**[Sourced via ch06 (reviewed) and the Pollard digest (in repo)]** The
return is now better-armed than the outline's sketch: Talbot at about
sixty-six (not the legend's eighty), who heard mass before the battle,
brought down with his horse before a fortified artillery park whose
budget line ch10 traced into the account books — "faulty intelligence,
momentum, honour, and the position of his unfurled banner" rather than
theatrical fatalism (Pollard, via the digest). The pattern restated
once, without the analogy overreaching: capital-intensive decisive
capability, requiring fiscal-administrative capacity, producing
consolidation, ending a dispersal. Not modernity, but Tudor — and the
book ends by handing the reader the indicators (Appendix C) with which
to prove it wrong.
