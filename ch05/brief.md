# Brief — Ch. 5: The Undermighty Kings (~11,000 words)
PURPOSE: Hollow sovereignty as pan-European condition. Henry VI treated
straight (incapacity 1453–54, loss of France, York's protectorate, Wars of
the Roses as symptom); Charles VI in retrospect (intermittent madness,
ducal regencies, Armagnac–Burgundian war, Troyes); Ottoman succession
crises of the 1440s (Murad II, devşirme placeholder). Modern payoff:
territorial hollow sovereignty (Somalia, Lebanon, Libya) vs. functional
(US procurement, EU defence, Japanese demography). The causal direction:
over-mighty subjects precede undermighty kings.
RESEARCH FOCUS: Griffiths, Watts, Vaughan, Famiglietti, İnalcık, Kafadar
(retrieval); Cerny on hollow sovereignty (bundled).
COMPARATORS: England, France, the Ottomans.
OBJECTION (critiques.md): the medical-specificity complaint — comparison at
the level of institutional response, not etiology.
