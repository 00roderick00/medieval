## T1 — Primary sources (open, consulted; key passages verified verbatim)

- **The Paston Letters**, ed. Gairdner (1904, 6 vols; public domain:
  archive.org; Project Gutenberg vols. I–III, VI) — the Gresham petition
  (vol. II: "riotous peple, to the nombre of a thowsand persones"; "not
  abille to sue the commone lawe… for the gret myght and alyaunce of the
  seid Lord"); Osbern to John Paston, May 1451 (vol. II no. 193: "the
  Kyng hath wrete to hym for to shewe favour to the Lord Moleyns");
  Paston's bill to the Chancellor ("mayntenaunces, champerties,
  embraceries").
- **The Golden Bull of 1356**, ch. 17 (Avalon Project, Yale) — the three
  days' public notice rule for lawful feud.
- **Imperial Reform (1495)**: the *Ewiger Landfriede* and
  Reichskammergericht texts, trans. Brady (GHDI) — "definitive abolition
  of the right of feud."
- **Götz von Berlichingen**, *Leben, Fehden und Handlungen* (1859 German
  ed., archive.org; first pub. 1731); GHDI English excerpt.
- **13 Ric. II c. 5 (1389)** (admiralty restriction) — via Judiciary UK
  history page and open scholarship.
