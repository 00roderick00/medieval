# Brief — Ch. 8: Private Justice, Private War (~11,000 words)
PURPOSE: The plural legal environment as the operative law of dispersal.
The Pastons as paradigmatic navigators (Castor; the Letters as T1); the
German Fehde and Landfrieden (Zmora; Götz von Berlichingen); Italian
vendetta and the signorie's street-level tolerance (Muir); sanctuary,
benefit of clergy, lex mercatoria, admiralty — medieval regulatory
arbitrage. Modern parallel: arbitration, ISDS, platform adjudication at
scale, offshore jurisdictions, the crypto legal experiment; Pistor as
bridge text. Close: royal justice as monopolist was a CONSEQUENCE of
fiscal-military consolidation — foreshadow ch. 11's canonical assertion.
RESEARCH FOCUS: Bellamy, Castor, Zmora, Muir, Pistor (retrieval); Cerny
sixth characteristic (bundled); contemporary arbitration/platform-court
reporting.
COMPARATORS: England, the Empire, Italy.
OBJECTION (critiques.md): the romanticisation complaint — inverted: the
plural environment was grim, its erosion an achievement, and its return
should be read in the same terms.
