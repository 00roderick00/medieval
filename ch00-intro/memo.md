## 1. The opening — Castillon, corrected and sourced

**[CONSENSUS, inherited from ch06 (reviewed) + digest]** 17 July 1453;
the Anglo-Gascon vanguard against Jean Bureau's entrenched artillery
park on the Lidoire; Talbot at about sixty-six, unarmoured by the terms
of his parole (digest-carried; verify on book), his horse brought down
by a round, killed on the ground; the army destroyed; Bordeaux
surrendered 19 October; the war conventionally over. The introduction's
framing sentence — the French had built "a state that can pay for
artillery" — is licensed by the reviewed record: the two permanent
budget lines (ordinaire/extraordinaire), the ~150-man salaried service,
artillery at 8% of military expenditure by 1489, Rogers's 1442 ratio,
and the reputational-surrender campaign of 1449–50. The draft may not
use: "sixty fortified places," the 300-gun flat count, Talbot-at-80, or
the guns-broke-the-line version of Formigny (all corrected in ch06).

## 3. The pendulum wager — the falsifiability passage (draft, numbered, quotable)

**[BOOK'S ARGUMENT — the passage the reviewers must be able to quote
verbatim; narrowed per §0.2; each condition carries its Appendix C
instrument.]** Draft text:

> The wager is falsifiable, and the reader should hold the book to it.
> If, over the next fifteen years:
>
> **1.** the decisive military layer proliferates — the number of
> polities able independently to field leading-node fabrication,
> proliferated military low-orbit reconnaissance, hyperscale sovereign
> compute, and a sustained peer-war munitions base rises materially
> above four; or a full-stack power is defeated in decisive engagement
> by cheap distributed systems fielded *without* a patron's
> intelligence, connectivity, and interceptor layers behind them
> [C6-1/C10-2; C12-1];
>
> **2.** the legibility infrastructure fragments in fact and not only
> in principle — bloc-internal fiscal and reporting reach falls, the
> untaxed share of offshore wealth rises again, and jurisdictionally
> plural, privacy-default identity and payment systems displace the
> bloc-scale stacks as the operative default [C11-2; C8-3];
>
> **3.** the platform universalisms consolidate independence instead of
> being absorbed — an actor combining decisive-relevant capability with
> a majority-commercial fiscal base escapes the state's instruments,
> and, decisively, resolves its disputes with states in fora states do
> not control [C2-3/C3-2; C11-3];
>
> **4.** the Tudor moves stall across all four candidate polities — no
> fiscal absorption of platform surplus, no canonical assertion that is
> enforced and upheld, no absorption of private force, no
> re-territorialisation of critical infrastructure [C11-1; C10-1;
> C4-1] — and, in the one candidate asserting canon without a fisc, the
> canon alone proves sufficient to consolidate a bloc [the Depreter
> test, chs. 10 and 12];
>
> — then the thesis fails, and the neomedievalists were right that the
> dispersal is stable. The book stakes itself on the opposite reading.

Notes for the draft: condition 4's final limb cuts *both* ways by
design — an EU consolidated by canon alone would falsify the mechanism
even while producing a settlement look-alike; the indicators named are
Appendix C's, where each carries a sourced baseline and directional
prediction; the fifteen-year horizon is the outline's, retained.

## 4. The three inoculations — with the verified texts

**Declinism-cycle (Kaplan). [T1]** The genre acknowledged in its
strongest instance, quoted exactly: "West Africa is becoming the symbol
of worldwide demographic, environmental, and societal stress, in which
criminal anarchy emerges as the real 'strategic' danger"; the "Last
Map... an ever-mutating representation of chaos"; and the stretch limo
— **Homer-Dixon's image, in Kaplan's mouth**: "Think of a stretch limo
in the potholed streets of New York City..." The answer is the
Roberts–Parker move stated as method: not worse vibes but a measured
price shift — Roberts's "great divide" (via Taylor 2019, open) applied
with ch06/ch10's discipline. The cycle objection is then conceded its
record and answered with the falsifiability passage: this iteration
publishes its own failure conditions.

**Fairyland (Rengger/Holsinger). [Access-honest per §0.3]** Rengger's
title question carried at summary level pending retrieval; Holsinger
quotable: neomedievalism as "the global idiom of the non-state actor,"
and IR neomedievalism as "a frightening lens on to the ultimate
co-optability of academic theorising into a regressive and destructive
political culture" (punctum-carried page cites; re-verify). The answer
is architectural, per the doctrine: the book does not analogise to a
fantasy Middle Ages; it specifies a mechanism operating in both
periods, sourced in the medieval case to the specialist historiography
at its most sceptical — **the sceptics-concede thread as the method
point** (the brief's note): Rogers supplies the
artillery→tax→centralisation spiral while ranking revolutions against
us; DeVries concedes France and Burgundy "conform" while attacking
determinism; Curry concedes the state-only threshold while reversing
the arrow; Cerny locates the original consolidation in "the creative
destruction of fifteenth to seventeenth century warfare." The
disagreements that remain are over ranking, tempo, and sequence inside
an agreed direction — the mechanism is assembled from the objections'
own concessions, which is the strongest available answer to the charge
of fantasy.
