## Objection 1 — The declinism cycle (steelmanned)

**Steelman.** Announcing the end of the state is a renewable licence.
Kaplan announced anarchy in 1994 and the decade delivered Amazon and the
WTO; Bull entertained new medievalism in 1977 and the decade delivered
the Second Cold War; Slaughter announced disaggregation in 2004 and the
decade delivered the surveillance state. The genre's tell is that its
evidence is always contemporary texture (this year's failed states, this
year's platforms, this year's drones) marshalled behind a timeless
pattern-claim — exactly this book's method. The pendulum is the genre's
perfect alibi: whatever happens is either the swing or the return swing.

**The introduction's answer.** Concede the genre and exit it by
construction. The book's claim is not textural but priced: a specific,
measurable shift in the cost of decisive capability, argued from account
books in the medieval case (the 1442 ratio; the 8%-of-military-spend
line; the two artillery budget lines) and from appropriations and
audited shortfalls in the modern one — the Roberts–Parker move, with
Roberts's "great divide" quoted and his critics staged, not hidden. And
the alibi is surrendered in print: the falsifiability passage (memo §3)
is a numbered, quotable, fifteen-year commitment with named indicators
and sourced baselines, including the condition (4, final limb) under
which even a settlement look-alike would falsify the mechanism.
Kaplan's essay could not fail; this book states how it fails. That is
the difference between a mood and a wager — and it is the entire reason
the passage is drafted as a block the reviewers can quote.

## Objection 2 — Fairyland (steelmanned)

**Steelman.** Rengger's question stands over the whole project: which
Middle Ages? The book's medieval half is a curated fifteenth century —
Bureau brothers but no plague, devşirme but no famine, legal pluralism
tidied into "jurisdiction" — a usable past assembled to license a
present-day thesis. Holsinger's charge lands harder: neomedievalism has
a political career, and its academic form is endlessly "co-optable into
a regressive and destructive political culture" — a book advising
insurers and sovereign funds that consolidation is coming is not exempt
from that career; it is an instance of it. The mechanism defence is no
defence: fantasies can have mechanisms too.

**The introduction's answer.** Two moves, both structural. First, the
book's medieval claims are hostage to the specialist historiography at
its most sceptical, not to a curated image: the mechanism is assembled
from the named concessions of the men who spent careers attacking its
strong versions — Rogers's own spiral, DeVries's France-and-Burgundy
concession, Curry's state-only threshold, Cerny's fifteenth-to-
seventeenth-century attribution — and the memos' correction record
(the dropped "sixty places," the demoted Formigny guns, the
Bureau-institution correction, the vendetta reformulation) shows the
fantasy being cut away wherever the sources demanded it. A fantasy does
not keep losing arguments with its own evidence. Second, Holsinger's
co-optation charge is answered by disclosure and falsifiability rather
than denial: the constituency is named in the coda, the implications
are fenced by doctrine, and the wager's failure conditions are printed
where the co-opters would have to read them. The book's residual
exposure — Rengger's chapter itself is not yet in hand — is declared in
sources.md rather than papered over: the fairyland question is engaged
at the strength the retrieved texts support, and no further.

## Objection 3 — English parochialism (steelmanned)

**Steelman.** Strip the comparative garnish and the book is an island
story: Castillon opens it, Cade riots in the middle, Henry VII's bonds
and Henry VIII's supremacy close it, and "Tudor" names the thesis. The
continental cases are load-balancers — a paragraph of Burgundy here, a
devşirme section there — while every structural beat (crisis,
consolidation, settlement) is keyed to English sources, English
statutes, English historiography. The dynastic accident objection
remains: England's fifteenth century was a contingent catastrophe, and
a pattern named after its winners is survivorship bias with footnotes.

**The introduction's answer.** The register is auditable, not asserted:
the mechanism's home case is France, not England — the taille, the
compagnies, the Bureau train, Louis XI (chs. 6, 10) — and England
enters Part II mostly as the *control case* (DeVries's exception: royal
gun monopoly without the fiscal substrate, collapsing into private
artillery war). The five moves are run four-ways with the Ottoman limit
case included precisely because the "New Monarchies" literature never
included it; the comparator sets are listed chapter by chapter in the
memo (§4) and tabulated in Appendix B. "Tudor" names the settlement's
*style* (personal-charismatic rule atop bureaucratic machinery;
absorption rather than destruction) because England documents that
style best — the naming convenience is conceded in the introduction,
and the pan-European simultaneity (Louis XI, the Catholic Monarchs,
Mehmed II) is the argument against the accident reading: four polities
do not have the same accident in the same generation.

## Objection 4 — The narrowed wager is a moved goalpost (from this pass)

**Steelman.** The introduction now states failure conditions that
differ from the outline's: "defeats concentrated capital" has grown a
patron-scaffolding qualifier; "privacy systems become the default" has
become a reach metric; the platform condition has acquired a
forum-migration clause. Each rewrite makes the thesis harder to
falsify by exactly the events most likely to occur (Ukrainian drone
success; encryption adoption; platform court victories). Tightening
language before publication is what motivated reasoning looks like
from the inside.

**The introduction's answer.** The direction of each change is
checkable against its research trail, and each replaces an
unmeasurable clause with a measurable one — which is the opposite of
insulation. "Defeats concentrated capital" was unscoreable (any drone
strike qualifies; no drone war settles it); the narrowed form names
the observable (decisive engagement without the patron layer) that the
March 2025 natural experiment showed to be the actual hinge — and it
can occur. "Becomes the default" was a vibe; bloc-internal reach is a
number with a baseline (C11-2, C8-3) that can fall. The
forum-migration clause *adds* a failure mode the outline lacked
(platform court victories inside state courts do not falsify;
adjudication leaving state courts does — and ch08 documents the
instruments by which it could). The original four conditions are
preserved in the outline, the narrowed passage is drafted in the memo
under review, and the delta is itself documented here — goalposts
moved in daylight, before kickoff, toward the opponent. If Roderick
prefers any original clause, the review is the place to restore it.
