# Brief — Ch. 1: The Return of the Middle Ages (~11,000 words)
PURPOSE: Establish the neomedieval diagnosis at book length; Bull's
hypothesis and Bull's caution; the c. 1200 pan-European baseline; Friedrichs'
Church/Empire duality as analytical engine; Cerny's six characteristics run
against the present with contemporary evidence per characteristic; Slaughter
as important-and-wrong; the state as the anomaly.
RESEARCH FOCUS: Bull ch. 10 close reading (retrieval); Friedrichs 2001/2007
(bundled); Cerny 1998, 2005 (bundled); Slaughter chs. 5–6 (bundled); Wight,
Strayer (retrieval). Contemporary evidence: current, sourced examples for
each Cerny characteristic.
COMPARATORS: the Rhineland and northern Italy c. 1200–1250 as the stated
baseline (per the concession below); the Papacy and Empire as the two
universalisms.
OBJECTION (critiques.md): the medievalist's flattening complaint — conceded
via the explicit Rhineland/northern-Italy baseline choice.
