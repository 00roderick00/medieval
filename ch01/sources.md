# Sources — Ch. 1: The Return of the Middle Ages

Graded per CLAUDE.md §5. Open-access items consulted for the memo carry URLs;
gated works are in the **RETRIEVAL LIST**, each naming the claim that depends
on it. Nothing on the RETRIEVAL LIST may be cited as consulted (§4). Where a
gated primary's formulation is verified via an open secondary that quotes it
with page numbers, that is noted — the formulation may be used; the primary
still goes to retrieval for direct quotation.

## T1 — Primary sources (open, consulted)

- **World Inequality Report 2022** (World Inequality Lab), wir2022.wid.world —
  wealth/income concentration figures (richest 10% = 76% wealth, 52% income).
- **UNCTAD Investment Dispute Settlement Navigator / news hub**,
  investmentpolicy.unctad.org — cumulative ISDS cases (1,332 by end-2023);
  Próspera v. Honduras (case 1292).
- **EU Commission, DMA gatekeepers portal**,
  digital-markets-act.ec.europa.eu — the six gatekeeper designations (6 Sep
  2023) and additions. Primary regulatory record.
- **EU Tax Observatory, Global Tax Evasion Report 2024**, taxobservatory.world
  — ~10% of world GDP held offshore; the honest "enforcement partly closing
  the gap since 2013" reading.
- **Pew Research Center, "Language and Traditions… National Identity" (2024)**,
  pewresearch.org — 36-country survey; declines in national-identity markers.
- *Underlying statutes as primary where the memo cites conflicts:* GDPR; US
  CLOUD Act; China PIPL (the "compliance trilemma").

## T2 — Canonical scholarship (open, consulted)

- **Mikael Baaz, "International Law in a 'New Medievalism'," *Scandinavian
  Studies in Law* 62: 117–141**, scandinavianlaw.se/pdf/62-6.pdf. **Open,
  peer-reviewed; read in full.** The load-bearing secondary for this chapter:
  quotes Bull (1977: 245, 254–266) and Friedrichs (2001: 475, 486–490)
  *verbatim with page numbers*. Used to source the Bull and Friedrichs
  formulations without consulting the gated primaries.
- **J. Gibbins, "The Death of Belonging?", *Social Evolution & History***,
  sociostudies.org — open; corroborates the Friedrichs Empire/Church→state/
  market translation.
- **Perry Anderson, *Lineages of the Absolutist State* (1974)**, pp. 37–38 —
  the "patchwork of overlapping and incomplete rights" formulation. Open via
  the ISEG Lisbon PDF (see reading-list.md).
- **J. G. Ruggie, "Territoriality and Beyond," *International Organization*
  47:1 (1993)** — heteronomy vs sovereignty; medieval parcelisation. Open PDF
  (ResearchGate, author article).
- **A. Osiander, "Sovereignty, IR, and the Westphalian Myth," *International
  Organization* 55:2 (2001)** — open full PDF (FES mirror). The "state as
  anomaly" anchor.
- **B. Teschke, *The Myth of 1648* (2003)** — open full text (libcom.org /
  Academia.edu, informal hosts; text authentic). Supports the sovereign state
  as a late, contingent settlement.
- **Cerny 2005, "Terrorism and the New Security Dilemma," NWCR 58:1**
  (bundled, corpus/bundled). The "war-college-tested" **five**-characteristic
  version and "durable disorder." *Note: not the six-fold list — see RETRIEVAL.*
- **Slaughter, *A New World Order* (2004), chs. 5–6** (bundled). The
  disaggregated-sovereignty optimist read (see corpus/bundled/index.md).

## T3 — Serious trade / synthesis / reputable reference (consulted)

- **medievalists.net** syntheses of Andrew Latham's work — modal medieval
  units; and Latham's "variation on a theme, not difference in kind" pushback
  (the named counter to the "state as anomaly" move).
- **worksinprogress.co**, "The Rise and Fall of the Hanseatic League";
  **Britannica**, "Hanseatic League" — the Hansa as near-sovereign commercial
  actor (with the nascent-c.1200 caveat).
- **Britannica**, "Guelf and Ghibelline"; **Met Museum** Heilbrunn timeline
  (Italian Peninsula 1000–1400) — northern-Italian fragmentation; the 1250–73
  Interregnum.
- **encyclopedia.com** (Holy Roman Empire); **New World Encyclopedia**
  (Innocent III) — the two universalisms (tertiary-encyclopedic).
- **CSIS; Foreign Policy; NDU (INSS)** — the great-power-competition / NATO
  re-consolidation material for characteristic 2 (contested).
- **Pistor, *The Code of Capital*** (Princeton UP page; Columbia Law summary)
  — IP/data as legally-coded capital (the book itself is the primary; used here
  as framing).
- **IISD**, investor–state / fossil-fuel ISDS report — supporting ISDS figures
  (note methodology differs from UNCTAD).

## RETRIEVAL LIST (gated — do NOT cite until the PDF is in the repo)

Each names the specific claim that depends on it. Access routes in
reading-list.md.

1. **Hedley Bull, *The Anarchical Society* (1977), ch. 10 + concluding
   chapter, pp. 245–266.** NEEDED FOR: the exact order and wording of Bull's
   five subsection headings, and his *verbatim* concluding-caution sentences
   (the "insufficient evidence" passage). Substance verified via Baaz (open);
   direct quotation requires the primary. (Open PDFs at olivialau.org /
   ndl.ethernet.edu.et were unreachable during research; Internet Archive
   borrow is the fallback.)
2. **Jörg Friedrichs, "The Meaning of New Medievalism," *EJIR* 7:4 (2001),
   pp. 475–476, 486–490** (or the 2007 Routledge chapter). NEEDED FOR: the
   sentence-level Empire/Church → nation-state-system/transnational-market
   duality, verbatim. Formulation verified via Baaz/Gibbins (open); primary
   for direct quotation.
3. **Philip G. Cerny, "Neomedievalism, Civil War and the New Security
   Dilemma," *Civil Wars* 1:1 (1998).** NEEDED FOR: the **six**-characteristic
   enumeration the outline runs (the bundled 2005 companion gives five). The
   chapter's central diagnostic instrument.
4. **Martin Wight, *Systems of States*, "De Systematibus Civitatum," pp.
   21–45.** NEEDED FOR: states-systems theory as the frame for "the state as a
   settlement, not a constant." (Archive borrow.)
5. **Joseph Strayer, *On the Medieval Origins of the Modern State* (1970).**
   NEEDED FOR: verbatim statements on the c. 1200 non-state and the four
   components of the state (persistence, impersonal law, bureaucracy, loyalty
   shift). Core argument available via open reviews; verbatim requires the
   borrow (archive.org / JSTOR).
6. **Colin Morris, *The Papal Monarchy: The Western Church 1050–1250*.**
   NEEDED FOR: a scholarly (not encyclopedic) statement that Innocent III
   marks the apex of the papal-monarchy claim.
7. **The Rhineland gap — RESOLVED to named candidates (dossier Part III,
   adopted on review 2026-07-24).** Immediate route: **Huffman, *Family,
   Commerce, and Religion in London and Cologne, c.1000–c.1300*** —
   **Cambridge alumni access confirmed**; begin at once
   (https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/family-commerce-andreligion-in-london-and-cologne/56B2D7D0665E816A16ACE9647929E513).
   Named acquisition candidates: **Huffman, *Medieval Cologne: From
   Rhineland Metropolis to European City (A.D. 1125–1475)*** (best exact
   geography; De Gruyter preview/purchase —
   https://www.degruyterbrill.com/document/doi/10.1515/9783111571140/html)
   and **Scales, *The Shaping of German Identity: Authority and Crisis,
   1245–1414*** (best conceptual defence against the flattening objection;
   test Cambridge Core —
   https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/shaping-of-german-identity/C6134B0992E7162F0636841FE9EC6CE2).
   NEEDED FOR: the claim — central to the chapter's answer to the
   flattening objection — that the Rhineland was *exceptionally*
   fragmented specifically c. 1250. Until one of these is read,
   northern-Italy-only remains the interim defence position (memo
   Revisions #2, #4).
8. **Philip Jones, *The Italian City-State*; Chris Wickham, *Sleepwalking into
   a New World*.** NEEDED FOR: a scholarly anchor for northern-Italian
   fragmentation and the 1250–73 Great Interregnum.
9. **Helen Nicholson or Alan Forey on the military orders.** NEEDED FOR: a
   scholarly statement of the orders as autonomous supranational political
   actors (open sources are popular only).
10. **Zielonka, *Europe as Empire* (2006)** — for the EU-as-neomedieval strand
   where ch. 1 touches it (developed fully in Ch. 11).
