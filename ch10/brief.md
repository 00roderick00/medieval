# Brief — Ch. 10: The Artillery State (~13,000 words)
PURPOSE: The book's central causal chapter. The compagnies d'ordonnance
(1445) in institutional detail; the taille's transition to permanence
(1439 Estates, Praguerie's defeat, élections and généralités); the Bureau
service as institution (maître de l'artillerie, arsenal, procurement
chains); Louis XI as the mature exemplar (Burgundy swallowed 1477,
appanages reduced, Commynes as T1); the Burgundian counter-case at full
weight — Charles the Bold's superb artillery lost to Swiss pikes, the
anti-determinism inoculation, and the deeper lesson: the Swiss won battles
without building the fiscal-administrative apparatus, so won no
consolidation. The chain compressed: technology → finance → sovereignty.
Modern sketch: the four candidate polities that can hold both stacks.
RESEARCH FOCUS: Contamine (definitive), Vaughan (Charles the Bold),
Kendall, Commynes, Rogers, Parker, Vale (retrieval); CHIPS Act, EU Chips
Act, Big Fund II, DoD industrial base assessments (primary, open).
COMPARATORS: France, Burgundy, the Swiss; England and the Ottomans
foreshadowed.
OBJECTION (critiques.md): the techno-optimist complaint (private financing)
— answered by the public financing of the military-relevant stack,
documented from the legislation itself.
