**Working file, not the finished appendix.** Per the coda brief, candidate
leading indicators are collected here from each chapter memo as it completes;
the finished Appendix C will consolidate them into 10–15 indicators, each
"measurable, dated, sourced, with baseline and directional prediction."

**Discipline (CLAUDE.md §9):** *no Appendix C indicator may be generated
without a source for its baseline.* Accordingly every candidate below carries
its baseline as **BASELINE: TO BE SOURCED**, with the intended primary source

The book's wager is falsifiable in one direction: if the military and
legibility stacks **decentralise** rather than concentrate, the thesis fails
(Introduction; CLAUDE.md §2). These indicators exist to make that testable.

## Candidates from Ch. 6 (1450: The Year the Walls Fell)

Chapter 6 supplies the *mechanism* — weapon capital-intensity concentrating
decisive force in the hands of whoever can pay — and, usefully, its
fifteenth-century *measurement form*. Rogers records that "as early as 1442,
the French government was spending more than twice as much on its artillery
train as… traditional war materiel," and that "the central governments of
large states could afford… their subjects and smaller neighbors… could not."
Each candidate below is the modern analogue of a Ch. 6 datum.

**C6-1 — Decisive-capability concentration ratio.**
- *Measures:* the number of polities able to *independently* field the full
  "decisive layer" (orbital ISR constellations at scale, leading-node
  foundries, hyperscale training compute, a sustained peer-conflict munitions
  industrial base).
- *Ch. 6 analogue:* only central crowns could afford a first-rate siege train;
  barons and towns could not.
- *Directional prediction:* if the count holds at ≤4 or falls, the
  concentration thesis holds; if it rises materially, the thesis is threatened.
- *BASELINE: TO BE SOURCED* — DoD industrial-base assessments; leading-node
  foundry capacity data (SIA/TSMC/Intel/Samsung); launch-cadence and satellite-
  count data; munitions production-rate reporting. (Ch. 10 / Ch. 12 will refine.)

**C6-2 — Public concentration of the military-relevant stack.**
- *Measures:* share of the military-relevant stack (as distinct from the
  civilian stack) financed by *permanent public* expenditure, concentrated in
  ≤4 polities.
- *Ch. 6 analogue:* the 1442 "twice as much on the artillery train" figure —
  decisive capability as a permanent line in the central fisc.
- *Directional prediction:* rising public concentration = mechanism operating;
  a shift toward distributed private/commodity financing = thesis threatened.
- *BASELINE: TO BE SOURCED* — CHIPS Act and EU Chips Act appropriations;
  Chinese "Big Fund" (I–III) disbursements; NDAA/defence-industrial
  appropriations. (Primary documents per CLAUDE.md §4; Ch. 10 owns this.)

**C6-3 — Capitulation-without-contest (the "surrender rather than resist"
analogue).**
- *Measures:* incidence of secondary polities aligning to a bloc's decisive-
  capability provider *without attempting independent capability* — basing and
  access grants, wholesale adoption of a bloc's compute/foundry/identity stack,
  export-control compliance — rather than contesting.
- *Ch. 6 analogue:* Rogers's finding that most of the hundred strongpoints
  "were never besieged; they surrendered rather than make a hopeless attempt to
  resist." The credible, unmatchable threat collapsed the order without a shot.
- *Directional prediction:* rising alignment-without-contest = the credible-
  threat mechanism operating (the strongest, most distinctive signal of the
  book's claim); rising independent capability-seeking among middle powers =
  thesis threatened.
- *BASELINE: TO BE SOURCED* — export-control regime adherence (e.g. US
  semiconductor controls and third-country compliance); sovereign-AI/sovereign-
  foundry dependency mapping; basing agreements. (Ch. 11 / Ch. 12 will refine.)

**C6-4 — Temporal-compression of position-collapse [WEAK — candidate for cut].**
- *Measures:* the speed at which a contested position (physical or
  infrastructural) can be rendered untenable by the decisive layer.
- *Ch. 6 analogue:* Bayeux's wall down in a sixteen-day siege; Blaye's in five.
- *Directional prediction:* n/a in isolation — too analogical and hard to
  operationalise; the 1940/2021 "temporal compression" material is atmospheric,
  not a metric.
- *Assessment:* flagged as the weakest candidate; likely **cut** unless a later
  chapter (Ch. 12, Ukraine 2022–24) supplies a defensible operational measure.
  Recorded here only so the running list is complete and the cut is logged, not
  silent (CLAUDE.md discipline on no silent truncation).
