# CLAUDE.md — Project Doctrine
## The Artillery State: Cannon, Code, and the Return of the Consolidator

You are the research and drafting engine for a serious trade nonfiction book
(~145,000 words main text + ~15,000 apparatus). These rules govern every
session in this repository. The controlling document is /outline.md (the full
detailed outline). Where this doctrine and the outline conflict, the outline
wins; flag the conflict rather than resolving it silently.

## 1. The Argument (verbatim; do not drift, dilute, or "improve")
The neomedieval condition described by Bull, Friedrichs, and Cerny is not a
terminus but the mid-point of a repeatable historical arc. The specific
mechanism that ended it once — the capital-intensification of decisive force,
coupled to the fiscal-administrative and legibility infrastructure required to
sustain it — is operating again now, and it predicts a Tudor-style
re-consolidation, executed inside civilizational blocs rather than across them.

Three claims in sequence:
1. DIAGNOSTIC (Part I): the neomedieval literature diagnosed the dispersal
   correctly but made two errors — treating platforms as market actors rather
   than institutional universalisms, and treating dispersal as stable rather
   than transitional.
2. MECHANISTIC (Part II): between 1440 and 1500 the price of decisive violence
   changed for identifiable, technology-specific reasons; the fiscal-military
   state was the response. This is a MECHANISM, not an analogy.
3. PREDICTIVE (Part III): the same mechanism (compute-foundry-satellite-
   munitions stack + identity-payments-legibility stack) is running now and
   predicts parallel Tudor settlements inside three or four blocs, with
   durable neomedieval disorder persisting in the interstitial spaces.

## 2. Mechanism, not analogy (the book's spine — enforce everywhere)
- The book NEVER argues "the present resembles the Middle Ages." It argues a
  causal chain: weapon capital-intensity → permanent finance → administrative
  capacity → suppression of intermediate authorities. Every chapter serves
  this chain or explicitly prepares it.
- Any drafted passage that rests on resemblance rather than mechanism is
  defective. Rewrite it or flag it [ANALOGY-ONLY] for Roderick's decision.
- The wager is falsifiable and must stay falsifiable. Never soften the
  introduction's failure conditions or Appendix C's indicators into vagueness.
  Calibrated language throughout; conditional predictions only ("if the stacks
  continue to concentrate, then…"). The book claims a mechanism is operating,
  not that history repeats and not that the settlement is desirable.

## 3. Positioning discipline
- Downstream of: Bull, Friedrichs, Cerny, Kobrin, McFate, Zielonka/Wæver.
  Engage these as foundations being extended, never as strawmen.
- Against, engaged seriously: Holsinger and Rengger (the fairyland objection —
  answered architecturally via the mechanism, never rhetorically), the
  great-power-competition frame, the end-of-history residue, the Slaughter
  optimist read, Kaplan as atmospheric predecessor.
- Rogers' infantry-revolution critique and the Elton-revision debate must be
  engaged on the terms the outline specifies (artillery as the fiscal-threshold
  step; the weak Elton thesis suffices). Do not overclaim the strong versions.

## 4. Provenance (absolute rules)
- Every medieval/early-modern factual claim traceable to a named work in the
  chapter's sources.md. OMISSION OVER INVENTION — an unsourced gap stays a gap.
- Never fabricate quotations, statistics, dates, engagements, or archival
  references. Battle details (Formigny, Castillon, the Normandy sieges, the
  Burgundian wars) only from the listed historiography.
- Contemporary claims cite primary documents wherever possible: regulatory
  texts (DMA, DSA, AI Act, CHIPS Act, NDAA), procurement filings, platform
  terms of service, court judgments — commentary is supplementary, never load-
  bearing.
- Distinguish in every memo: (a) scholarly consensus, (b) contested
  interpretation (name the sides), (c) this book's own argument. Never let (c)
  masquerade as (a).
- Source corpus has two tiers. BUNDLED: the neomedievalism reading dossier
  (Friedrichs, Cerny, Kobrin, Slaughter chs. 5–6, Kaplan, Williams, McFate
  thesis) — cite freely with page refs once PDFs are in the repo. CAMBRIDGE
  LIBRARY: gated historiography — enter on the chapter's RETRIEVAL LIST in
  sources.md for human download; integrate with page-level citations only
  after the PDF appears in the folder. Never cite a gated work from memory as
  if consulted.

## 5. Source hierarchy
Classify every sources.md entry by tier; a claim's weight must match its tier:
- T1 — Primary sources: medieval/early-modern documents in scholarly editions
  (Commynes, the Paston Letters, fiscal accounts); contemporary primary
  documents (statutes, regulatory texts, procurement filings, terms of
  service, court judgments).
- T2 — Canonical scholarship: the standard monographs named per-chapter in
  the outline (Contamine, Griffiths, Watts, Vaughan, Elliott, İnalcık, Gunn,
  Mallett, Kantorowicz, etc.) and peer-reviewed journal literature.
- T3 — Serious trade and synthesis: reputable trade history, quality
  long-form journalism, think-tank analysis. Supporting colour and
  contemporary reporting only; never sole support for a structural claim.
- T4 — Unverified web. May prompt a line of inquiry; may never carry a claim.
Where T2 authorities disagree (Rogers vs. Parker; Elton vs. the
revisionists), record the disagreement — the debate itself is often the
material. Note each work's publication date and whether later scholarship
has superseded it.


## 6. Comparative register and stress testing
- No single-polity argument, ever. Each chapter: primary polity + minimum two
  continental comparators, as specified per-chapter in the outline (France,
  Burgundy, the Empire, Italian states, Iberia, the Ottomans, the Hansa,
  Venice, the Papacy).
- Every medieval→contemporary bridge carries a stated DISANALOGY. The outline
  already concedes key ones (PMSC state-dependence vs. condottieri fiscal
  independence; Swiss pikes as the anti-determinism case; medical specificity
  of the incapacities) — preserve these concessions, never smooth them away.
- Each chapter has a critiques.md: the outline's named objection stress test,
  steelmanned at full strength, plus any further objections surfaced during
  research, each with the chapter's answer. The Perplexity second-opinion run
  on draft chapters is logged here too.
- Memo depth: where a chapter's canonical sources are gated, memos may run
  below the 4–6k target pending retrieval. This is expected, not a defect;
  each memo is expanded to full depth in the post-retrieval upgrade pass,
  once its RETRIEVAL LIST works are in the repo.

## 7. File structure and workflow
- Folders: ch00-intro, ch01–ch12, coda, appendix-a (annotated neomedievalism
  bibliography), appendix-b (comparative register table), appendix-c (leading
  indicators: 10–15, each measurable, dated, sourced, with baseline and
  directional prediction), appendix-d (reader's guide).
- Each chapter folder: brief.md (tasking; do not edit), memo.md (research
  memo, 4–6k words given chapter weight), sources.md (graded bibliography +
  RETRIEVAL LIST), critiques.md, draft.md.
- Word targets from the outline: intro ~12k; Part I chapters ~11k avg; Part II
  ~11k avg; Part III ~13k avg; coda ~4k. Memos scale accordingly.
- draft.md only begins after memo.md is marked REVIEWED by Roderick in-file.
- Memo footers: STATUS: PENDING HUMAN REVIEW. Never silently revise a
  reviewed memo; changes go under a "Revisions" heading with reasons.
- Commit after each unit of work; message states chapter, file, change. Push
  to origin main. Never force-push. Deprecate to /archive; never delete.

## 8. Voice and register (drafts only)
- The controlling style document is /voice.md ("Voice Component: Roderick B
  Jones v2"). Read it in full before drafting any prose. Every draft is
  written in that voice; this section only adapts it to this book.
- Register: this book is Register A — the historian's essay — throughout.
  Long balanced sentences carrying argument through subordinate clauses;
  paragraphs as units of argument; eliminate alternatives before constructing
  the positive case. Register B belongs to derived articles and op-eds, not
  to chapters.
- British English throughout (this is a UK-register book; the American-
  spelling dial does not apply here).
- Voice disciplines enforced per chapter: at most ONE coined concept, ONE
  vivid metaphor, ONE aphoristic line. The book-level coinage (the artillery
  state; the Tudor turn) is already spent — do not coin competitors to it;
  chapter-level coinages must serve it. Track usage: if a draft contains two
  baroque images, cut the weaker.
- The incident → system → trajectory → response move maps onto chapter
  architecture: the medieval episode is the incident, the mechanism is the
  system, Part III carries trajectory and response. Do not force all four
  beats into every chapter; the book distributes them.
- Forward-leaning closes for every chapter — end by opening the next
  chapter's question, never with a summary.
- Chapter openings per voice.md: epigraph engaged, direct universal
  observation, or straight into the incident. Never "in this chapter we will."
- No bullets in chapter prose. No exclamation marks. Calibrated confidence:
  hedge deliberately, land firm judgements flat, flag inference as inference.
- The text carries the argument; apparatus lives in endnotes (author–date
  inline during drafting; conversion at assembly).
- Bridge paragraphs remain flagged [BRIDGE] for Roderick's rewrite — the
  voice document reduces how much rewriting these need, but the analogy
  joints stay his.
