# Sources — Ch. 7: Cade and the Communes

Graded per CLAUDE.md §5. Open items consulted carry URLs; gated works in the
**RETRIEVAL LIST** with dependent claims (§4). Notable: a genuine T1 primary
(the Complaint, open on Wikisource) and an unusually strong open peer-reviewed
base on the modern side (N&I working paper read in full; five PR journal
articles). Several key figures are BLOCKED pending manual verification —
listed at the foot.

---

## T1 — Primary sources (open, consulted)

- **"The Complaint of the Poor Commons of Kent" (c. 4 June 1450)** — full
  Middle English text, Wikisource (from Stow, *Three Fifteenth-Century
  Chronicles*). The rebels' own manifesto; quoted directly in the memo.
- **Ordonnance of 2 November 1439** (Orléans Estates) — the royal troop
  monopoly + taille; content verified via Herodote.net / France-Histoire-
  Espérance / Britannica (the ordonnance text itself in *Ordonnances des
  rois de France* — retrieval if quoted verbatim).
- **OBR, Economic and Fiscal Outlook, March 2024** — UK tax take "close to a
  post-war high of 37.1% of GDP by 2028-29." obr.uk.
- **Lord Ashcroft referendum-day poll (24 June 2016, N=12,369)** — Leave
  composition and stated reasons ("take back control" 49%).
  lordashcroftpolls.com.
- **Treaty of Tübingen (1514) / Armer Konrad records** — via Landesarchiv
  Baden-Württemberg (institutional primary-adjacent).
- **B. Brogden Orridge, *Illustrations of Jack Cade's Rebellion* (1869)** —
  public domain, archive.org (Guildhall records; does not reproduce the
  manifestos in the retrievable portion).

## T2 — Canonical scholarship (open, consulted)

- **Inglehart & Norris, "Trump, Brexit, and the Rise of Populism" (HKS
  RWP16-026, 2016)** — open PDF, **read in full**; the petty-bourgeoisie
  finding quoted verbatim; the Lipset/Bell lineage. (Working paper — flag;
  the peer-reviewed fuller statement is *Cultural Backlash*, gated.)
- **Green & Pahontu, "Mind the Gap: Why Wealthy Voters Support Brexit,"
  *BJPS* 54 (2024)** — 73% of Leave voters homeowners; wealth as
  self-insurance. cambridge.org.
- **Manza & Crowley, "Working Class Hero?", *The Forum* 15 (2017)** — open
  PDF (NYU); Trump primary affluence, peer-reviewed corroboration of the
  Silver figures.
- **Fetzer, "Did Austerity Cause Brexit?", *AER* 109 (2019)**; **Margalit,
  "Economic Insecurity and the Causes of Populism, Reconsidered," *JEP* 33
  (2019)**; **Funke, Schularick & Trebesch, "Populist Leaders and the
  Economy," *AER* 113 (2023)**; **Douenne & Fabre, "Yellow Vests,
  Pessimistic Beliefs, and Carbon Tax Aversion," *AEJ:EP* 14 (2022)** — all
  AEA-hosted, abstracts/articles open.
- **Girardi, "Precarious work and challenger parties," *IPSR* 54 (2024)** —
  the M5S contradiction. **Lengfeld (*KZfSS* 2017) / Lux (*KZfSS* 2018) /
  Lengfeld reply (2018)** — the AfD exchange, cited as an exchange.
- **Mudde, "The Populist Zeitgeist," *G&O* 39 (2004)** — the thin-centred
  definition (page verified via Cambridge/Oxford front matter).
- **Chamorel, "Macron Versus the Yellow Vests," *J. of Democracy* 30
  (2019)** — gated; abstract/excerpt verified.
- **Hanover Historical Review (Kitchel), "The Ciompi Revolt of 1378"** —
  open PDF (undergraduate journal — T2/T3 boundary; use for narrative, not
  load-bearing interpretation).
- **Rouban (CEVIPOF) and Gonthier et al. via The Conversation** — the gilets
  jaunes survey findings (researcher-authored summaries of CEVIPOF/ANR
  surveys; T2-adjacent).
