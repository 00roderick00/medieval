# Brief — Ch. 7: Cade and the Communes (~11,000 words)
PURPOSE: The propertied-anxious revolt as recurring European form. Cade
1450 (grievances, composition, conservative demands — Harvey); the
Praguerie 1440 as revolt against the fiscal-military state itself; the
Ciompi legacy and absorption by the signorie (Najemy); the German pattern
(Bundschuh, urban revolts, 1525 foreshadowed). Structural features of the
form; the modern parallel (2010s–20s populist wave via Mudde/Norris/
Inglehart) at pattern level; the critical asymmetry — revolts shape but do
not prevent consolidation.
RESEARCH FOCUS: Harvey, Watts, Contamine, Najemy, Brady/Oberman/Tracy
(retrieval); populism political science (open/retrieval).
COMPARATORS: England, France, Florence, the Empire.
OBJECTION (critiques.md): the trivial-or-misleading complaint — the
comparison is the cross-case pattern of revolt-under-consolidation, not
Cade-to-Trump.
